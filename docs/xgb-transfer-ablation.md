# XGBoost Transfer Ablation: Can More Finetuning Undo the Damage?

The XGBoost RLM-transfer model on PAMPA drops to near-random performance
(0.509 AUC-ROC, 0.853 AUC-PR vs 0.855 random baseline). The README
hypothesizes this is because inherited RLM decision trees permanently bias
predictions and cannot be overcome by continued boosting. This ablation
tests that hypothesis directly.

## Hypothesis

If the failure is due to **insufficient finetuning rounds** (the model
just needs more rounds to undo the RLM predictions), then increasing the
finetuning budget should recover performance toward the scratch baseline.

If the failure is **structural** (inherited trees permanently contribute
to predictions and cannot be overridden), no amount of additional boosting
will fully recover scratch performance.

## Experiment

We varied two parameters across the full 25-fold CV on PAMPA:

- **Pretrain rounds** (number of boosting rounds on RLM): 50, 200
- **Finetune budget** (maximum boosting rounds on PAMPA): 200, 500, 1000

All configurations use early stopping (patience=20 rounds on validation
loss), so the effective number of new rounds may be less than the budget.

## Results

| Configuration | AUC-ROC (mean ± std) | AUC-PR (mean ± std) | Effective new rounds |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Scratch (budget=200)** | **0.684 ± 0.062** | **0.917 ± 0.024** | **58** |
| Transfer (pretrain=50, budget=200) | 0.583 ± 0.078 | 0.878 ± 0.031 | 95 |
| Transfer (pretrain=50, budget=1000) | 0.587 ± 0.085 | 0.879 ± 0.032 | 95 |
| Transfer (pretrain=200, budget=200) | 0.527 ± 0.081 | 0.860 ± 0.030 | 112 |
| Transfer (pretrain=200, budget=500) | 0.529 ± 0.084 | 0.860 ± 0.031 | 117 |
| Transfer (pretrain=200, budget=1000) | 0.523 ± 0.077 | 0.858 ± 0.031 | 119 |

## Interpretation

**The failure is structural, not budget-limited.** Increasing the
finetuning budget from 200 to 1000 rounds (5x) produces no meaningful
improvement for either pretrain configuration:

- pretrain=200: AUC-ROC goes from 0.527 → 0.523 (no change, within noise)
- pretrain=50: AUC-ROC goes from 0.583 → 0.587 (no change, within noise)

Early stopping confirms this: despite having budgets of 500 or 1000, the
models only add ~95-119 new rounds before validation loss stops improving.
The model converges to a bad solution regardless of budget.

**More pretrain rounds make the problem worse.** The transfer models with
200 pretrain rounds (0.523-0.529 AUC-ROC) perform consistently worse than
those with 50 pretrain rounds (0.583-0.587). More inherited RLM trees
means more RLM-specific decision boundaries to overcome, and the model
has a harder time compensating.

**The damage is irrecoverable.** Even the best transfer configuration
(50 pretrain, 1000 budget) recovers only to 0.587 vs 0.684 scratch — a
persistent -0.097 AUC-ROC gap despite 5x the finetuning compute. The
scratch model achieves its performance with only 58 effective rounds,
while transfer models use 95-119 new rounds and still fail.

## Mechanism

XGBoost's `xgb_model` transfer adds new trees on top of existing ones.
The existing RLM trees still contribute to every prediction: each
prediction is the sum of ALL tree outputs (original + new). New trees
can learn to partially compensate for wrong RLM predictions by outputting
corrections, but they cannot delete or modify the existing trees. This
means the RLM signal is always present in the output, and new trees must
allocate capacity to correction rather than learning PAMPA patterns from
scratch.

This is fundamentally different from D-MPNN transfer, where replacing the
FFN head creates a clean slate for the task-specific decision layer while
retaining only general molecular representations from the encoder.
